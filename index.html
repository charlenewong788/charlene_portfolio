Profile

Researching environments.
Designing with light.

My work sits at the intersection of environmental research and lighting design. With a background in electrical and electronic engineering, I use environmental sensing, simulation and data analysis to understand how light performs in buildings—and how it influences comfort, sleep and wellbeing.

My earlier research explored daylight, glare and smart-window strategies in learning environments, leading to a peer-reviewed journal paper and a Best Abstract Award. At UCL’s Institute for Environmental Design and Engineering, I now investigate the relationship between personal daylight exposure and sleep through wearable sensing and field research.

I translate research evidence into thoughtful, measurable and human-centred lighting strategies.

Outside research and design, you’ll usually find me on a fencing piste. En garde.

Current research

Mapping personal daylight exposure and sleep quality using wearable sensors

How does personal light exposure change as people move through everyday environments? In summer 2026, I conducted a field study with approximately 18 office-based participants at UCL East.

The study combines wearable light sensing with activity, physiological and indoor environmental data to map variations across time, place and daily routine. Participants wear a LYS light sensor, an Empatica E4 wristband and a Garmin activity tracker, while an Atmo Pro records indoor environmental conditions.

Together, these measurements build a real-world picture of the light people receive and the contexts in which exposure occurs. The analysis examines how time of day, activity and environmental conditions contribute to differences in personal light exposure.

The aim is to translate field evidence into practical daylight and electric-lighting strategies for more responsive, human-centred homes and workplaces.

SUPERVISED BY DR AHMET BAHADDIN ERSOZ & DR MANDANA SAREY KHANIE · UCL IEDE

Selected work

W-01

CONTINUUM · modular exposure monitoring

2026 · UCL IEDE · PROJECT RESEARCHER

An interdisciplinary UCL project developing a modular wearable system to monitor personal exposure to light, alongside noise, air quality, temperature and physiological signals across everyday environments.

As part of the research team, I support the pilot across the study lifecycle—from equipment preparation and sensor checks to deployment and field data collection. I also participate in discussions around data quality, multi-sensor integration and analysis planning, gaining practical insight into the Python-based workflows used to organise, harmonise and time-align recordings.

W-02

Smart classroom windows for daylight & glare

2024–2025 · PUBLISHED RESEARCH · PROJECT RESEARCHER

How should smart windows behave so students get daylight without the glare? A study of daylight needs and controllability in Asian universities, developed into an optimisation proposal for classroom window systems. Published in Wiley’s International Journal of Mental Health Nursing; the conference abstract won the Best Abstract Award at ICDAMI 2024.

See publications P-01 / C-01

W-03

Tooth segmentation with BiSeNet

2023 · IEEE · ISHC 2023 · PROJECT LEADER

First-author exploration of real-time semantic segmentation for dental imagery using the Bilateral Segmentation Network, presented at the 5th International Symposium on Smart and Healthy Cities and published by IEEE.

DOI 10.1109/ISHC61216.2023.00026

W-04

Apollaxis · Reclaiming the Light

2026 · COMPETITION ENTRY · LIGHT OF TOMORROW

A proposal reimagining the Grade II listed gasholder at Bromley-by-Bow as a public place shaped by sunlight. A kinetic facade shifts between closed, half-open and open states through a low-energy counterweight system, letting daylight organise atmosphere, wayfinding and the rhythm of the day; rooftop solar panels store bright days for London’s grey ones.

Competition board · PDF

W-05

Dancing with Daylight · a house by the sea

DESIGN PORTFOLIO · 20 BOARDS · SOLE DESIGNER

A daylight-driven private house stepped down a coastal slope, with daylight as the primary organiser of space, circulation and mood. The portfolio carries the project from solar site analysis and massing studies through plans, sections and a 35-view daylight simulation matrix to interior light studies and physical models.

Full portfolio · PDF

W-06

Light | Shadow · lighting design for Mysteria

2026 · UCL BENV0190 · RIBA STAGE 3 · LIGHTING DESIGNER

An integrated lighting proposal for a glass Christmas decoration maker near Krakow: two facades, the landscape and a multi-purpose hall, treating shadow as a design material. Grasshopper daylight analysis to BS EN 17037, SLL Code for Lighting 2022 criteria, DIALux verification and DALI-2 daylight-linked scene control, within a EUR 100,000 budget.

Full report · 17 A3 boards · PDF
